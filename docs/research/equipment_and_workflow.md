## 1. Torres de Escoramento (Shoring Towers)

### 1.1 When to Use Towers vs Individual Props (Escoras Telescopicas)

**Individual Props (Escoras Telescopicas):**
- Best for standard residential/commercial slabs up to ~4.6m height
- Simple flat slabs with moderate loads
- Quick setup for small areas
- Cost-effective for low heights and light loads
- Typical capacity: 500 kg (open) to 1,800 kg (closed) depending on model

**Shoring Towers:**
- Required when height exceeds ~4.5m (practical limit of telescopic props)
- Heavy loads: thick slabs, transfer beams, bridge decks
- When lateral stability is critical (wind loads, asymmetric loading)
- Infrastructure projects: bridges, viaducts, industrial buildings
- Loads from 6 to 26+ tons per tower depending on system

**Decision Criteria:**
| Factor | Props | Towers |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Height | Up to 4.6m | 4m to 30m+ |
| Load per point | Up to 1,800 kg | 6,000 to 26,000+ kg |
| Setup complexity | Low | Medium-High |
| Cost per m2 | Lower for simple slabs | Higher but necessary for heavy loads |
| Lateral stability | Requires bracing | Built-in with diagonals |
| Reuse flexibility | High | High (modular) |

### 1.2 Major Tower Systems in Brazil

#### SH System - Torres de Carga LTT

**LTT (Load Tower):**
- Modulations: 0.75 x 1.25 m up to 1.00 x 2.50 m
- Maximum load capacity: 12 tons (120 kN) per tower
- Suitable for any height within modular limits
- Components: Cruzeta (cross-brace), Sapata Regulavel (adjustable base), Garfo (fork head), Garfo Regulavel (adjustable fork), Quadro (frame)
- Applications: industrial, commercial, residential, bridges, viaducts
- Assembled in 1.50m vertical sections forming rigid portals

**LTT Extra (Extra Load Tower):**
- Double the load capacity of standard LTT
- Designed for heavy construction: bridges, viaducts, industrial structures
- Same modular assembly principle with reinforced frames
- Used when standard LTT capacity is insufficient

**SH Trusses (Trelicas):**
- Models: SH100, SH200, SH300
- Spans up to 30m
- Used for bridges, viaducts, building cantilevers, pedestrian bridges
- Allow large clear spans without intermediate support

**SH VM Beams:**
- VM130: Primary distribution beam, height 130mm -- used as main beam (longarina) in slab/beam shoring
- VM80: Secondary beam, height 80mm -- used as secondary beam (barrote)
- VM50: Lighter secondary beam for residential/light commercial
- These sit atop towers/props and distribute load to the formwork panels

#### ROHR System - ETEM Tower

- Maximum load: up to 26 tf (metric tons) per tower
- Vertical load: 6.50 tf per upright (for towers up to 5m)
- Base configurations: 100x100 cm, 100x150 cm, or 150x150 cm
- Maximum height: up to 5m standard
- Construction: galvanized steel trapezoidal frames with male-female terminals
- Key feature: four-face post bending triples load capacity
- Can form "mesas deslocaveis" (movable tables) for cycle work
- Work platform load: 150 kg/m2

#### PERI Systems

**PEP Ergo (Slab Props):**
- Not a tower but PERI's primary prop system
- Maximum capacity: 50 kN
- Compliant with EN 1065 Classes B, D, E
- Models and capacities:

| Model | Height Range | Weight | Load Capacity |
|-------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| PEP Ergo D-150 | 0.96 - 1.50 m | 9.1 kg | 30 kN |
| PEP Ergo D-250 | 1.47 - 2.50 m | 13.1 kg | 35 kN |
| PEP Ergo D-300+ | 1.79 - 3.00 m | 15.9 kg | 35 kN |
| PEP Ergo D-350+ | 2.08 - 3.50 m | 19.2 kg | 40 kN |
| PEP Ergo D-400 | 2.51 - 4.00 m | 23.2 kg | 40 kN |

- Full galvanization for durability
- Load-to-weight ratio up to 70% better than comparable props

**PERI UP Flex (Shoring Tower):**
- Up to 100 kN per leg
- Modular design: 25 cm and 50 cm increments
- Gravity Lock technology for safe assembly
- Designed for thick slabs and infrastructure
- Lightweight ergonomic components

**ST 100 Stacking Tower:**
- PERI's heavy-duty tower system
- Stackable modular design
- Used for heavy infrastructure loads

#### ULMA Systems

**MK Heavy-Duty Tower:**
- 6 tons per post (demonstrated in Rodoanel project, Sao Paulo)
- Compatible with H700 and DW530 special profiles
- Used in major infrastructure: Belo Monte hydroelectric, Rodoanel
- Rail-mounted for movable configurations

**ALUPROP (Aluminum Props):**
- Aluminum construction for lightweight handling
- Can form towers up to 12m high
- Certified load capacity
- Ideal for projects requiring frequent repositioning

#### Cuplock System
- Connection via cup-and-blade mechanism
- Single hammer blow locks four components
- Load capacity ~15% higher than traditional clip-type scaffolding
- Widely used internationally, growing adoption in Brazil
- Standard spacing: cups at every 500mm interval

#### Ringlock (Rosette) System
- 8-hole rosette connection welded to standards
- Allows horizontal bars at multiple angles
- Ring at every 500mm interval
- Versatile: can serve as both scaffolding and shoring
- Major manufacturers: Layher, Hunnebeck/Brand, various Chinese manufacturers

### 1.3 Configuration Rules

**Base Plates (Sapatas):**
- Always required on solid, level surface
- Adjustable base plates (sapatas regulaveis) for fine height adjustment
- Must distribute load to ground without exceeding soil bearing capacity
- Minimum 150x150mm base plate for standard towers
- Larger base plates (200x200mm+) for heavy loads or soft ground

**Diagonal Bracing (Contraventamento):**
- Mandatory in both directions (X-bracing)
- Must be present on all four faces of tower for full lateral stability
- Spacing per manufacturer specifications (typically every 1.5m vertical)
- Critical for wind load resistance and prevention of progressive collapse

**Height Limits:**
- Individual props: maximum ~5m (practical limit with stability)
- SH LTT towers: modular, limited by load/slenderness ratio
- ETEM towers: up to 5m standard
- PERI UP Flex: designed for medium to tall heights
- Heights > 8m typically require engineering review per NBR 15696
- Heights > 20m require special engineering for wind and stability

**General Rules:**
- All towers must have horizontal bracing at top and bottom
- Adjacent towers must be tied together with horizontal members
- Free-standing tower height-to-base ratio should not exceed 4:1 without additional bracing
- Ground preparation essential: compacted, drained, able to support point loads
- Load distribution beams (sole plates / pranchoes) under base plates on soil

### 1.4 How Towers Are Specified in Engineering Projects

In the DXF/drawing, the engineer typically specifies:
- Tower plan position (X, Y coordinates or grid references)
- Tower base modulation (e.g., 1.00 x 1.50 m)
- Tower system/brand (e.g., SH LTT, ETEM, PERI UP)
- Height to underside of beam/slab
- Load per tower (derived from tributary area calculation)
- Bracing requirements and tie points
- Base plate / sole plate requirements
- Primary beam (longarina) direction and spacing
- Secondary beam (barrote) direction and spacing
- Formwork panel layout

### 1.6 Cost Comparison: Rental vs Purchase

**Rental (Locacao):**
- Telescopic props: ~R$ 40/unit/month (2026 reference)
- A typical 120 m2 slab needs ~45 props = ~R$ 1,800/month
- Tower systems: higher monthly rate but includes engineering support
- Advantages: no storage costs, no maintenance, flexibility per project, technical assistance included
- Most common model for construction companies

**Purchase:**
- Higher upfront investment
- Requires storage space and maintenance program
- Makes sense for companies with continuous high volume
- Break-even typically at 12-18 months of continuous use
- Risk of equipment obsolescence

**Factors affecting price:**
- Quantity (volume discounts)
- Rental duration (monthly contracts cheaper per day)
- Equipment model and capacity
- Distance/freight to job site
- Additional services (delivery, assembly, engineering)

## 2. Vigas de Distribuicao (Distribution Beams)

### 2.1 Beam Hierarchy in Slab Shoring

The shoring system follows a clear structural hierarchy:

```
Concrete Slab
    |
Formwork Panels (compensado fenolico / chapa metalica)
    |
Secondary Beams (barrotes / transversinas) -- VM50, VM80
    |
Primary Beams (longarinas) -- VM130, GT24, H20
    |
Tower Head / Prop Fork (garfo / forcado)
    |
Towers or Props (escoras)
    |
Base Plates (sapatas)
    |
Ground / Foundation
```

### 2.2 SH System Beams

**VM130 (Viga Metalica 130mm):**
- Height: 130 mm
- Used as primary beam (longarina)
- Steel construction, high load capacity
- Typical spans: up to 2.5-3.0m between supports (depending on load)
- Standard lengths available from SH catalog
- Most commonly used primary beam in the SH system

**VM80 (Viga Metalica 80mm):**
- Height: 80 mm
- Used as secondary beam (barrote)
- Lighter than VM130
- Typical spacing: 400-600mm depending on slab thickness and load
- Sits perpendicular to VM130 on top

**VM50:**
- Height: 50 mm
- Lightest metal beam option
- For light residential applications
- Used as secondary beam in low-load scenarios

### 2.3 PERI GT24

- Lattice girder (truss beam) design
- Standard lengths: various, including 5.90m
- High load capacity due to triangulated lattice structure
- Conforms to EN standards
- Used as primary or secondary beam depending on configuration
- Lighter than solid web beams of equivalent capacity

### 2.4 Doka H20

**H20 Beam Family:**
- Models: H20 top, H20 eco (variants N and P)
- Technical specifications:
  - Bending moment (M): 5 kNm
  - Shear force (Q): 11 kN
  - Stiffness (E x J): 450 kNm2
  - Weight: 4.4 kg/lfm (N variant) | 5.3 kg/lfm (P variant)
- Conforms to EN 13377
- Wooden/composite construction with steel flanges
- Worldwide standard for formwork beams
- Available in various standard lengths

### 2.5 ULMA BTM System

- Simple and practical shoring beam system
- Few components for easy site application
- Components: BTM beams, bidirectional heads, simple heads, plywood panels, struts, tripods
- Quick assembly and disassembly

### 2.6 Wood vs Metal Beams

| Aspect | Wood Beams | Metal Beams (VM/GT24/H20) |
|--------|-----------|--------------------------|
| Cost | Lower initial cost | Higher initial cost |
| Durability | Limited reuse cycles (5-15) | 100+ reuse cycles |
| Load capacity | Lower, variable (defects) | Higher, consistent |
| Weight | Lighter per unit | Heavier but stronger |
| Precision | Less precise | Manufactured tolerance |
| Availability | Easy to source locally | Requires rental/purchase from supplier |
| Fire resistance | Flammable | Non-combustible (metal) |
| Sustainability | Renewable but disposable | Reusable, recyclable |
| Engineering | Harder to guarantee properties | Certified load tables |
| Common use | Small residential projects | Commercial, industrial, infrastructure |

**When to use wood beams:**
- Small residential projects
- Budget-constrained works
- Remote locations where metal beams are unavailable
- Temporary/one-time use scenarios

**When to use metal beams:**
- Any project requiring engineered shoring (NBR 15696)
- Reusable system economics favor metal over 3+ projects
- Higher loads, longer spans
- Professional rental company supply chains

### 2.7 Connection Details

**Beam-to-Tower Connection:**
- Tower head piece (cabecote) receives primary beam
- Adjustable fork (garfo regulavel) allows fine height adjustment
- U-shaped fork holds beam in position, prevents lateral displacement
- Some systems use drop-head mechanism for early stripping

**Beam-to-Prop Connection:**
- Forcado (fork head) on top of prop receives beam
- Simple forcado: single beam support
- Double forcado: can support beam intersection
- Cruzeta (cross piece): supports beam bottom at an angle for beam formwork

## 3. Andaimes para Escoramento (Scaffolding for Shoring)

### 3.1 Types of Scaffolding Used in Construction

#### Andaime Fachadeiro (Facade Scaffolding)
- Primary purpose: worker access on building exteriors
- NOT designed for shoring loads
- Composed of vertical frames and planks
- Maximum load: typically 150-200 kg/m2 (access load only)
- Should NEVER be used as shoring support

#### Andaime Tubular (Tube and Clamp Scaffolding)
- Versatile tube-and-coupler system
- Can be configured for light shoring in some cases
- Limited load capacity compared to dedicated shoring towers
- Requires engineering verification when used for shoring
- Common in renovation/retrofit projects

#### Andaime Multidirecional (Multidirectional Scaffolding)
- Most advanced and versatile system
- Types: Cuplock, Ringlock (Rosette), Layher Allround
- 8-hole rosette allows connections at multiple angles
- Can serve dual purpose: access AND shoring
- Higher load capacity than traditional scaffolding

### 3.2 Multidirectional Systems Comparison

| System | Connection | Load per Leg | Key Feature |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Cuplock | Cup-and-blade | ~15% higher than clip | Single hammer lock |
| Ringlock | Rosette (8-hole) | Up to 22 kN/leg | Multi-angle connections |
| Layher Allround | Rosette | High | Premium quality |
| PERI UP | Gravity Lock | Up to 100 kN/leg | Tool-free assembly |

### 3.3 Shoring vs Access Load Capacities

**When used for ACCESS:**
- Typical design load: 150-200 kg/m2 (1.5-2.0 kN/m2)
- Light duty (inspection): 75 kg/m2
- Heavy duty (masonry): 300 kg/m2
- Safety factor: typically 4:1

**When used for SHORING:**
- Design loads: 2,000-10,000+ kg per leg
- Must be specifically engineered per NBR 15696
- Requires diagonal bracing in all planes
- Base plates on solid ground or sole plates
- Height-to-width ratio limitations apply
- A 3-layer multidirectional system can support up to ~36 tons (79,475 lbs) with safety factor 4:1

### 3.4 When to Use Scaffolding as Shoring

- When the structure geometry is irregular (non-rectangular plan)
- Curved structures requiring variable spacing
- When access AND shoring are needed simultaneously
- Renovation projects where traditional towers cannot fit
- When the system is already on site for facade work (economy of scale)
- ALWAYS requires engineering verification and approval

## 4. Escoramento Lateral (Lateral Shoring / Formwork)

### 4.1 Formas para Muros de Arrimo (Retaining Wall Formwork)

**Steel Panel Systems:**
- Modular steel panels with standardized dimensions
- Surface: steel plate backed by welded steel grill frame
- Connected with pins, wedges, or bolts
- Can be assembled to any wall height and length
- Typical panel sizes: 300x1200mm, 600x1200mm, 900x1200mm, etc.
- Surface options: steel, plywood facing, melamine laminate

**Tie Systems:**
- Through-ties (tirantes passantes) connect opposite form faces
- Coil ties, she-bolts, or dywidag bar systems
- Spacing determined by lateral concrete pressure calculation
- Cone spacers maintain wall thickness
- PVC water stops at tie locations for water-retaining structures

**Alignment and Bracing:**
- Struts (escoras de travamento) brace forms against wind and concrete pressure
- Walers (travessas) distribute tie forces along panel face
- Push-pull props (escoras regulaveis) for vertical alignment
- Must resist full hydrostatic pressure of fresh concrete

### 4.2 Formas para Cortinas / Paredes Diafragma (Diaphragm Walls)

- Typically formed with single-sided formwork
- Concrete cast against excavated soil face (guide walls)
- Steel reinforcement cage placed before concreting
- Specialized equipment: hydromill, clamshell grab
- Formwork mainly for the cap beam / top of wall

### 4.3 Escoramento de Valas e Trincheiras (Trench Shoring)

**Legal Requirements:**
- Brazilian regulations: excavations deeper than 1.25m REQUIRE shoring when soil is susceptible to collapse
- NR-18 (worker safety) mandates protection systems

**Blindagem Metalica (Steel Trench Shields):**
- Two parallel steel walls connected by adjustable struts (estroncas)
- Strut width varies based on pipe/utility diameter
- Can use 3 or 4 walls forming a containment box
- Can be stacked (overlapped) for greater depth
- Lowered into trench by excavator
- Applications: sewer, water, drainage pipeline installation

**Pontaletes Metalicos (Steel Struts):**
- For small/medium depth trenches
- Adjustable length to span trench width
- Ideal for point repairs and maintenance
- Simpler than full shield systems

**Components:**
- Steel wall panels (chapas de blindagem)
- Adjustable hydraulic or mechanical struts (estroncas)
- Guide rails (trilhos-guia)
- Stacking connectors for deep excavations
- Safety functions: prevents trench collapse, protects workers

**Key Suppliers:**
- ArcelorMittal (blindagem de vala products)
- Pro Escoramentos (specialized trench shoring)
- VPA Infra (trench and slope solutions)

### 4.4 Paineis Metalicos (Steel Formwork Panels)

**Types of Contact Surface:**
- Plywood (compensado plastificado/resinado): most common, 10-15 reuses
- Phenolic plywood (compensado fenolico): 20-50 reuses, better finish
- Steel plate: 200+ reuses, best finish, heavy
- Aluminum plate: light, good finish, expensive
- PVC/plastic sheets: light, limited reuses

**Panel Construction:**
- Welded steel grid frame (grelha soldada)
- Can be aluminum or steel frame
- Standardized dimensions for modular assembly
- Quick-connect hardware (cunhas, pinos, borboletas)

### 4.5 Lateral Pressure Calculation (NBR 15696 Annex D)

The lateral pressure of fresh concrete on vertical formwork is calculated per NBR 15696 Annex D, based on DIN 18218 adapted for Brazilian conditions (reference temperature 25 C).

**Base Formula:**
```
Pb = 12 * Vb + 12
```
Where:
- Pb = pressure of fresh concrete (kN/m2)
- Vb = rate of rise of concrete (m/h) -- velocity of concreting

**Factors Affecting Lateral Pressure:**

| Factor | Effect on Pressure |
|--------|-------------------|
| Higher casting speed (Vb) | INCREASES pressure |
| Higher temperature | DECREASES pressure (faster cure) |
| Lower temperature | INCREASES pressure (slower cure) |
| Concrete with retarders | INCREASES pressure |
| Self-compacting concrete (SCC) | INCREASES pressure (near hydrostatic) |
| Vibration | INCREASES pressure |
| Slump/workability | Higher slump = higher pressure |

**Correction Coefficients:**
- Weight-specific correction coefficient (gamma correction)
- Chemical composition and admixture coefficient
- Temperature coefficient
- The 2023 revision of NBR 15696 added self-compacting concrete (CAA) to the pressure charts

**Distribution:**
- Pressure increases linearly from top (zero) to a maximum value
- Maximum pressure occurs at a depth related to casting speed
- Below maximum depth, pressure remains constant (unlike pure hydrostatic)
- For very slow casting, maximum pressure < full hydrostatic
- For very fast casting or SCC, approaches full hydrostatic

**Design Implications for Escora.AI:**
- Wall thickness does NOT directly affect concrete pressure (common misconception)
- The form design must resist the calculated pressure regardless of wall thickness
- Tie spacing is the primary variable adjusted based on pressure calculation

### 4.6 Sistemas de Escoramento de Fachada (Facade Bracing)

- Used during construction to stabilize newly cast walls
- Push-pull props (escoras regulaveis) at angles
- Connected to wall via cast-in anchors or through-bolts
- Temporary: removed after concrete reaches design strength
- Particularly important for tilt-up (pre-moldado) construction

## 5. Cimbramento (Falsework)

### 5.1 Definition and Distinction

**Escoramento vs Cimbramento:**
- **Escoramento (Shoring):** General term for any temporary support during construction. Focuses on vertical load transfer.
- **Cimbramento (Falsework):** Specifically the complete temporary structure that supports formwork for casting concrete in elevated or spanning structures. Includes shoring + framework + formwork as an integrated system.
- **Reescoramento:** Props placed after initial formwork removal to continue supporting young concrete while it gains strength. Cheaper system, only needs to carry dead load.

### 5.2 Cimbramento Convencional (Conventional Falsework)

**Composition:**
1. **Support (Apoio):** Props and/or towers
2. **Framework (Ossatura):**
   - Primary beams (longarinas/vigas primarias)
   - Secondary beams (barrotes/vigas secundarias/transversinas)
3. **Formwork surface (Superficie moldante):** Plywood panels or steel panels
4. **Accessories:** Connectors, adjusters, bracing

**Typical Assembly (bottom to top):**
```
Ground -> Base plates -> Props/Towers -> Fork heads ->
Primary beams (longarinas) -> Secondary beams (barrotes) ->
Plywood panels -> Concrete
```

**Applications:**
- Standard building slabs (most common)
- Building beams
- Short-span bridge decks
- Industrial structures

### 5.3 Cimbramento Especial (Special Falsework)

**Torres de Cimbramento para Pontes (height > 10m):**
- Heavy-duty tower systems (SH LTT Extra, ULMA MK, PERI)
- Require detailed engineering design
- Wind load analysis critical at height
- Foundation design for concentrated tower loads
- Progressive collapse analysis required
- Typically steel frame towers with cross-bracing on all faces
- Load capacity: 6-26+ tf per tower leg

**Key Considerations for Tall Towers:**
- Slenderness ratio limitations
- Plumbness tolerance (typically L/600)
- Settlement monitoring during loading
- Staged loading procedures
- Temperature effects on steel members

### 5.4 Cimbramento Auto-Lancavel (MSS - Movable Scaffolding System)

**What it is:**
A self-launching steel structure that serves as both falsework and formwork carrier for span-by-span bridge construction. Once a span is cast and stressed, the MSS advances to the next span position without requiring ground support.

**Types:**
- **Overhead MSS:** Supported from above on launching girders
- **Underslung MSS:** Supported from below, hanging beneath the bridge deck

**Capabilities:**
- Spans up to 55m
- Can cast both double-beam and box-deck cross sections
- Includes steel structure, working platforms, internal/external formwork
- Hydraulically operated
- Eliminates need for ground-level falsework

**Brazil Experience:**
- M30x2-S system used on Rodoanel (Sao Paulo) -- considered unprecedented in Latin America at the time
- Brought by Contern company
- Allows superstructure concreting within the equipment itself
- No ground contact needed -- critical for crossing obstacles (rivers, roads, valleys)

**When to Use MSS:**
- Multi-span bridges/viaducts with repetitive cross-section
- Sites where ground support is impractical (water, traffic, deep valleys)
- Projects with tight schedules (cycle time: typically 7-14 days per span)
- When total deck area justifies the high equipment cost

### 5.5 Other Special Falsework Systems

**Balanco Sucessivo (Successive Cantilever / Free Cantilever Method):**
- Used by SH and ROHR in Brazil
- For bridges/viaducts in reinforced or prestressed concrete
- Concrete cast in balanced segments from each pier
- Form traveler advances along completed segments
- Used when obstacles prevent ground-level falsework
- Segments typically 3-5m long, cast alternately on each side

**Cimbramento de Arcos (Arch Falsework):**
- Specialized curved falsework for arch bridges
- Requires custom-fabricated curved members or adjustable systems
- Support from ground or temporary towers at arch springing points
- Removed (decentered) after arch reaches design strength

## 6. Equipamentos Auxiliares (Auxiliary Equipment)

### 6.1 Garfos e Forcados (Fork Heads)

**Forcado Simples (Simple Fork):**
- U-shaped metal piece on top of prop
- Holds and aligns single beam (wood or metal)
- Available for various beam sizes
- Steel construction, painted or galvanized finish

**Forcado Duplo (Double Fork):**
- Supports beam intersection or wider beams
- Used at beam crossing points
- Distributes load across wider bearing area

**Garfo de Madeira (Wood Fork):**
- Traditional wooden fork for beam support
- Being replaced by metal forcados in professional systems
- Protection covers available (Metroform product)

### 6.2 Chapas de Base e Cabecotes (Base Plates and Head Pieces)

**Sapata Regulavel (Adjustable Base Plate):**
- Threaded rod with flat plate at bottom
- Allows fine height adjustment from below
- Essential for leveling on uneven ground
- Distributes point load to ground surface
- Typical adjustment range: 100-300mm

**Cabecote / Cabecal (Head Piece):**
- Top fitting on prop or tower leg
- Receives primary beam
- May include height adjustment (screw thread)
- Drop-head models allow early stripping of formwork while maintaining shoring

### 6.3 Cruzetas e Diagonais (Cross Pieces and Diagonals)

**Cruzeta (Cross Piece / Triangle Support):**
- Also called "triangulo de apoio"
- Supports the bottom of beam formwork
- Attached to props to hold beam form sides
- Critical for beam shoring (vs slab shoring)
- Creates the "channel" shape needed for beam formwork

**Diagonais / Contraventamento (Diagonal Bracing):**
- X-pattern bracing between tower frames
- Provides lateral stability to prevent racking
- Mandatory component in all tower systems
- Pin-connected for easy assembly/disassembly

### 6.4 Tripes (Tripods)

- Holds prop vertical during assembly
- Three-legged support clamps to prop tube
- Reduces labor needed during setup (prop stands alone)
- Essential safety accessory for prop erection
- Prevents props from falling during assembly sequence

### 6.5 Vigas Primarias e Secundarias em Escoramento de Laje

**Primary Beams (Longarinas / Vigas Primarias):**
- Run in the main spanning direction
- Supported directly by props or tower heads
- Typical spacing: 1.0-2.5m (depending on load and beam capacity)
- Types: VM130, GT24, H20, wood beams (sarrafos 6x16cm, 6x12cm)

**Secondary Beams (Barrotes / Vigas Secundarias / Transversinas):**
- Run perpendicular to primary beams
- Support formwork panels directly
- Typical spacing: 400-600mm
- Types: VM80, VM50, H20, wood beams (sarrafos 2.5x7cm, 5x7cm)

### 6.6 Compensadores e Extensores (Compensators and Extenders)

- Fill gaps between standard modular dimensions
- Compensator pieces for non-standard spacing
- Extenders for reaching heights between standard prop lengths
- Important for achieving exact slab elevation

### 6.7 Paineis de Laje (Slab Panels)

**Compensado Plastificado/Resinado (Plastic-coated Plywood):**
- Most common formwork panel in Brazil
- Thickness: typically 12mm, 14mm, or 18mm
- Standard sheet: 1100 x 2200mm or 1220 x 2440mm
- Reuse: 5-15 cycles depending on care
- Low cost, easy to cut for openings

**Compensado Fenolico (Phenolic Plywood):**
- Higher quality, smoother finish
- Film-faced with phenolic resin
- Reuse: 20-50 cycles
- Better concrete surface finish
- Higher cost per sheet
- Preferred for exposed concrete (concreto aparente)

**Chapa Metalica (Steel Deck / Metal Panel):**
- Reusable 200+ times
- Best surface finish
- Heaviest option
- Used in repetitive industrial/commercial projects
- Higher initial cost, lowest cost per use over lifetime

## 7. Fluxo de Trabalho Real (Real Workflow)

### 7.1 How the Engineer Specifies Shoring Today (Manual Process)

**Current State of Practice:**

1. **Structural Engineer** designs the concrete structure (slabs, beams, columns)
   - Produces structural drawings (planta de formas)
   - Specifies concrete strength (fck), slab thickness, beam dimensions
   - Provides loading information

2. **Formwork/Shoring Engineer** (often at the rental company or specialized firm) designs the temporary works:
   - Reviews structural drawings
   - Calculates loads (dead weight of concrete + construction live load)
   - Selects equipment type (props, towers, beams)
   - Produces shoring layout drawings
   - Specifies quantities (BOM - Bill of Materials)

3. **Manual Process Pain Points:**
   - Time-consuming: 2-5 days for a typical floor plan
   - Error-prone: manual load calculations, manual equipment selection
   - Iterative: changes in structure require re-doing shoring design
   - Knowledge-dependent: relies on experienced engineer/technician
   - No standardization between companies (each has their own methods)

### 7.2 Antiprojeto vs Projeto Executivo

**Antiprojeto (Preliminary Design / Pre-project):**
- Quick estimate of shoring needs
- Based on "taxa de escoramento" (shoring rate) in kg/m3 or R$/m2
- Used for budgeting, bidding, and initial planning
- Approximate quantities and equipment types
- May specify general system (e.g., "escoras telescopicas" vs "torres")
- Typically a 1-2 page document or simple spreadsheet
- Produced in hours, not days

**Contents of Antiprojeto:**
- Total slab area (m2)
- Estimated number of props or towers
- Primary and secondary beam quantities (linear meters)
- Panel quantities (m2)
- Approximate equipment list for cost estimate
- General notes on system type and limitations

**Projeto Executivo (Detailed Design / Construction Drawing):**
- Complete engineering design per NBR 15696
- Detailed layout drawing showing every prop/tower position
- Load calculations for each support point
- Equipment specifications with exact models and sizes
- Assembly/disassembly sequence
- Safety requirements and inspection checklist
- Signed and stamped by responsible engineer (ART)
- Required by Brazilian law for all commercial/professional construction

**Contents of Projeto Executivo:**
- DXF/CAD drawings with prop/tower positions
- Load calculation memorial (memorial de calculo)
- Bill of Materials (lista de materiais) with exact quantities
- Assembly sequence and procedures
- Stripping/removal sequence (ordem de desforma)
- Re-shoring plan if applicable
- Safety notes per NR-18 and NBR 15696
- ART (Anotacao de Responsabilidade Tecnica)

### 7.3 Taxa kg/m3 (Shoring Rate per Cubic Meter)

**What it is:**
A simplified metric used in the antiprojeto phase to estimate the amount of shoring material (by weight) needed per cubic meter of concrete in the structure.

**How it is used in practice:**
- The engineer uses a reference rate (e.g., 0.25 kg/m2 of slab area for basic formwork consumables)
- For structural steel in beams: ~150 kg/m3 of concrete
- For retaining walls: ~50 kg/m3 of concrete
- Different rates for different structural elements (slabs, beams, columns, walls)
- Rates vary by project type (residential vs commercial vs infrastructure)
- Used to produce quick cost estimates for bidding

**Limitations:**
- Very approximate -- actual quantities can vary 20-50%
- Does not account for specific geometry, heights, or load conditions
- Different companies use different reference rates
- Not suitable for final pricing or equipment allocation
- Experienced estimators adjust rates based on project specifics

### 7.4 How Rental Companies (Locadoras) Prepare Proposals

**Typical Workflow at a Locadora:**

1. **Receive Request:**
   - Client sends structural drawings (planta de formas)
   - Client specifies floor heights, slab thickness, beam sizes
   - Client may request specific system or ask for recommendation

2. **Technical Analysis:**
   - Engineer/technician reviews drawings
   - Calculates total areas, beam lengths, heights
   - Selects appropriate equipment from available stock

3. **Stock Optimization:**
   - Check available inventory against project needs
   - Optimize equipment selection based on what is in stock
   - Substitute equivalent equipment when preferred items unavailable
   - Balance between ideal engineering solution and stock reality
   - Consider equipment return dates from other projects

4. **Commercial Proposal:**
   - Equipment list with quantities
   - Monthly rental rates per item
   - Minimum rental period (typically 30 days)
   - Delivery and collection charges (freight)
   - Optional: assembly/disassembly labor
   - Optional: engineering design services (projeto de escoramento)
   - Insurance and liability terms
   - Replacement/damage charges

5. **Delivery and Support:**
   - Equipment delivery and site unloading
   - Optional: assembly supervision or full assembly crew
   - Periodic inspection during rental period
   - Equipment collection after stripping

**Key Pain Points for Locadoras:**
- Manual stock checking across multiple warehouses
- No automated system to match project needs to inventory
- Difficult to optimize across multiple concurrent projects
- Equipment tracking and maintenance scheduling
- Return condition assessment and damage billing
- Engineering design bottleneck (limited technical staff)

### 7.5 Role of BIM in Modern Shoring

**Current State:**
- BIM adoption in Brazil growing but still limited for temporary works
- Most shoring design still done in 2D AutoCAD
- Some large companies (SH, PERI, Doka) offer BIM libraries for their products
- Revit and Tekla are the primary BIM platforms used in Brazil

**BIM Benefits for Shoring:**
- Clash detection between shoring and permanent structure
- Automatic quantity take-off
- 4D scheduling (shoring erection/removal sequence)
- Integration with structural analysis
- Visual communication with site teams
- Coordination with MEP and other trades

**BIM Limitations for Shoring:**
- BIM objects for Brazilian shoring systems are limited
- Most rental companies do not work in BIM
- Small/medium engineering firms lack BIM capability
- Temporary works are often the last discipline to be modeled
- ROI unclear for one-off projects

**Opportunity:** A tool that bridges the gap between 2D CAD input and intelligent shoring design, potentially generating BIM-ready output, would fill a significant market gap.

### 8.4 Antiprojeto to Projeto Executivo Transition

**How to handle this in the software:**

**Phase 1 - Antiprojeto Mode:**
- Input: structural drawing (DXF) + basic parameters (height, slab thickness)
- Processing: automatic layout with default rules and safety margins
- Output: approximate BOM, cost estimate, general layout
- Speed: minutes, not days
- Accuracy: +/- 20-30% (acceptable for budgeting)

**Phase 2 - Projeto Executivo Mode:**
- Input: refined parameters, specific equipment selection, detailed loads
- Processing: full NBR 15696 calculation, optimization
- Output: complete engineering package (drawings + calculations + BOM)
- Speed: hours, not days
- Accuracy: engineering-grade (must be signed by responsible engineer)

**Transition Flow:**
1. Engineer creates antiprojeto for budgeting
2. Project is approved, moves to execution phase
3. Same model is refined into projeto executivo
4. Equipment is ordered based on final BOM
5. Changes during construction trigger recalculation

**Key Insight:** The antiprojeto-to-executivo transition is where most time is wasted today. If the tool maintains a single model that progressively refines, the engineer avoids duplicated work.

## Appendix A: NBR 15696 Key Requirements Summary

**Scope:** Design, dimensioning, and execution procedures for formwork and shoring for cast-in-place concrete structures.

**Key Load Combinations:**
- Dead load: weight of fresh concrete (typically 25 kN/m3) + formwork self-weight
- Construction live load: minimum 1.0 kN/m2 (workers, equipment)
- Impact/dynamic load: minimum 0.5 kN/m2
- Wind load: per NBR 6123
- Concrete lateral pressure: per Annex D formula

**Safety Factors:**
- Material safety factors per material type
- Load factors for permanent and variable actions
- Minimum safety factor against overturning: 1.5

**Inspection Requirements:**
- Equipment inspection before each use cycle
- Assembly verification by qualified technician
- Load testing for critical applications
- Periodic re-inspection during extended use
- Documentation and records

**2023 Revision Key Changes:**
- Updated lateral pressure calculations (Annex D)
- Added self-compacting concrete provisions
- Updated load combination criteria
- Aligned with updated Eurocode references
